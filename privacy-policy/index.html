Overview

PasswordScan is a fully client-side web application. All password analysis occurs entirely within your web browser using JavaScript. No password data is ever transmitted to our servers or any third-party service.

We are committed to complete transparency about what data we do and do not collect.

Data We Collect

We collect no personally identifiable information. There are no accounts, no sign-ups, and no user profiles. We do not collect names, email addresses, IP addresses, or any other identifying information.

If we integrate privacy-focused analytics in the future, we will collect only aggregate, anonymized data such as page view counts and general country of origin — never individual user data, and never any password-related data.

Your Passwords — Most Important

The passwords you type into PasswordScan are never transmitted, stored, logged, or seen by anyone other than you. The analysis is performed entirely by JavaScript running locally in your browser.

You can verify this yourself: open your browser's Developer Tools (F12), go to the Network tab, and type a password. You will see zero outgoing network requests containing your password.

When you close or refresh the page, any password you typed is gone immediately.

Cookies

PasswordScan does not use cookies or any persistent local storage. We do not track you across sessions or sites.

Third-Party Services

We use Google Fonts to load the Inter typeface. This results in a request to Google's servers to download the font file. Google's privacy policy governs this interaction. No password data is involved in this request.

We do not use advertising networks, tracking pixels, social media buttons, or any other third-party scripts that could profile you.

Contact

If you have any questions about this privacy policy, you can reach us by opening a GitHub issue on our public repository.
